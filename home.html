U.S. Department of the Treasury

Offices

The United States Treasury is composed of many offices, each responsible for a different job. Their overall function is to run and operate the department of the Treasury.

Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, and Accessibility Implements programs to promote diversity, equity, inclusion, and accessibility.
Domestic Finance Develops policies and assists in areas regarding domestic finance, banking, and other economic matters that relate.
Economic Policy Assists in the creation and evaluation of current and potential economic policies, both domestically and internationally.
General Counsel Provides legal and policy advice within the department.
International Affairs Focuses on international economic affairs in hopes of strengthening the U.S. economic environment.
Legislative Affiars Interacts with legislation to improve the state of the department.
Management Responsible for managing the entire department internally, including the monitoring of the budget, human resources, technology, etc.
Financial Research Improves the quality, transparency, and accessibility of financial data and information by conducting research.
Financial Stability Created to help stabilize and recover the U.S. economy.
Public Affairs Handles strategy and execution of communicating policy to the public.
Recovery Programs Leads the department’s implementation of economic relief, much like the Financial Stability Office.
Tax Policy Develops and implements tax policy programs, negotiates tax treaties, develops international and domestic tax policy.
Terrorism and Financial Intelligence Develops the government’s strategies to combat financial terrorist attacks.
Treasurer of the United States Oversees the Bureau of engraving and printing.
Tribal and Native Affairs Works with tribal governments and indigenous communities to promote economic prosperity.
